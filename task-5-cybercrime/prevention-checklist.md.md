# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: Checklist for College Students in India

## Essential Prevention Tips

### 1. **Protect Your UPI PIN & OTP**
   - ✓ NEVER share your UPI PIN, password, or OTP with anyone—not even bank staff
   - ✓ Always cover the keypad when entering sensitive credentials in public
   - ✓ Remember: Your bank will NEVER ask for your PIN or OTP via call/message
   - ✓ Memorize your PIN; don't write it down or save it in notes

### 2. **Verify Payment Requests Before Confirming**
   - ✓ Check the merchant name carefully before approving any UPI payment request
   - ✓ Ask the shopkeeper verbally to confirm the exact amount before scanning QR codes
   - ✓ Never scan QR codes from unknown sources or suspicious messages
   - ✓ Screenshot transaction receipts for all payments over ₹1,000

### 3. **Use Strong, Unique Passwords**
   - ✓ Create passwords with 12+ characters mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
   - ✓ Use different passwords for each account (email, bank, social media, gaming apps)
   - ✓ Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts
   - ✓ Change passwords every 3 months for banking/financial apps

### 4. **Identify & Avoid Phishing Attacks**
   - ✓ Be suspicious of emails/messages asking to "verify your account" or "confirm details"
   - ✓ Check sender email addresses carefully (scammers use similar names like "amajon.com")
   - ✓ Never click links in unsolicited messages; go directly to official websites
   - ✓ Legitimate banks won't ask for personal info via email or SMS

### 5. **Secure Your Devices**
   - ✓ Install and regularly update antivirus software on your phone/laptop
   - ✓ Use screen locks (PIN/pattern/biometric) on all devices
   - ✓ Enable automatic lock after 5 minutes of inactivity
   - ✓ Download apps ONLY from official Play Store/App Store, not third-party sources

### 6. **Be Cautious on Public WiFi**
   - ✓ NEVER access your bank account or UPI apps on public WiFi networks
   - ✓ Disable auto-connect features for WiFi and Bluetooth
   - ✓ Use a VPN (Virtual Private Network) if you must use public networks
   - ✓ Avoid shopping, password changes, or sensitive transactions on campus/cafe WiFi

### 7. **Recognize Social Engineering Scams**
   - ✓ Be wary of "prizes," "free money," or "lottery winnings" you didn't enter
   - ✓ Don't trust calls claiming to be from your bank offering loans/credit
   - ✓ Scammers often create urgency ("Your account will be blocked!") to rush you
   - ✓ Always call your bank's official number (from your card) to verify claims

### 8. **Monitor Your Accounts Regularly**
   - ✓ Check your bank statements and UPI transaction history weekly
   - ✓ Set up SMS/push notifications for all transactions above ₹500
   - ✓ Report suspicious activity immediately; don't wait
   - ✓ Keep a list of all your financial accounts and review them monthly

### 9. **Be Smart About Personal Information Online**
   - ✓ Don't share your Aadhaar, PAN, date of birth, or address on social media
   - ✓ Be careful with "fill out this form to win" contests—they collect personal data
   - ✓ Limit what you post about your location, schedule, or home address
   - ✓ Adjust privacy settings on Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp to "Friends Only"

### 10. **Know Your Rights & Report Cybercrime**
   - ✓ If you're a victim of cybercrime, report it immediately—don't feel ashamed
   - ✓ Keep evidence: screenshots, transaction records, message logs, dates/times
   - ✓ File an FIR (First Information Report) at your local police station
   - ✓ Contact cyber police units in your state for investigation support

---

## 🚨 **How to Report Cybercrime**

### **National Cyber Crime Portal**
- **Website**: https://cybercrime.gov.in
- **Process**: 
  1. Visit the portal and click "Report Incident"
  2. Provide details about the crime (type, date, amount, suspect info)
  3. Upload evidence (screenshots, transaction details, chat logs)
  4. Receive acknowledgment and tracking reference number
  5. Portal forwards complaint to relevant law enforcement agencies

### **Helpline 1930 (National)**
- **What it is**: 24/7 cybercrime helpline by Ministry of Home Affairs
- **How to use**: 
  - Call **1930** from any phone (toll-free)
  - Available in English and Hindi
  - Operators can guide you through the reporting process
  - Get immediate assistance for urgent cyber threats
  - File complaints for all cybercrime types

### **Additional Reporting Channels**
- **State Cyber Police**: Contact your state's cybercrime unit (search "Cyber Police [State Name]")
- **Bank Fraud Helpline**: Call your bank's dedicated fraud reporting number
- **FIR Registration**: Visit your nearest police station with evidence

---

## ⚠️ **What NOT to Do If You're a Victim**

- ❌ Don't panic or ignore the incident—act immediately
- ❌ Don't delete evidence (screenshots, messages, transaction records)
- ❌ Don't make further payments to recover lost money (it's usually a scam)
- ❌ Don't share your case details on public social media (compromises investigation)
- ❌ Don't contact the scammer again or try to negotiate

## 💡 **Quick Reference: Who to Contact**

| **Situation** | **Contact** | **When** |
|---|---|---|
| UPI/Payment fraud | Your bank (24-hour helpline on card) | Immediately |
| Cybercrime complaint | National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) | Within 24 hours |
| Helpline/Guidance | 1930 Helpline | Anytime (24/7) |
| Local police | Local police station / Cyber Police | For FIR registration |
| Phishing/Malware | Google (myaccount.google.com/security) or Apple ID Security | Within hours |

## 📱 **Student-Specific Tips**

- ✓ Don't share your college ID or student account password
- ✓ Beware of "campus placement scams" offering fake job opportunities
- ✓ Don't download assignments or notes from untrusted sources (malware risk)
- ✓ Never lend your phone/laptop to others; personal data can be accessed
- ✓ Be cautious of cyberbullying on college groups; report and block harassers

---

**Remember**: Your digital safety is YOUR responsibility. When in doubt, ask a trusted adult, your bank, or official authorities before taking action.
